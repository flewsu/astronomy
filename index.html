What is astronomy?

Astronomy is the study of objects and matter outside the earth's atmosphere and of their physical and chemical properties.

Geocentric model

In astronomy, the geocentric model (also known as geocentrism, or the Ptolemaic system) is a description of the cosmos where Earth is at the orbital center of all celestial bodies. This model served as the predominant cosmological system in many ancient civilizations such as ancient Greece including the noteworthy systems of Aristotle and Ptolemy. As such, they believed that the Sun, Moon, stars, and naked eye planets circled Earth.
